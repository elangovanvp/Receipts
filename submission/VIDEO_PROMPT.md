# Receipts — Demo Video Production Package

> _For the World Product Day 2026 hackathon (sub-3-minute demo video requirement). Contains an AI text-to-video master prompt, per-shot image prompts, a voiceover script, and a screen-recording fallback._

*World Product Day 2026 ("Everyone Ships Now") · Mind the Product × Pendo · 16:9 · ~120 seconds*
*Rallying line: "No source, no claim." · CTA: "Free. No login. Get receipts."*

---

## 0. Production Notes (read first)

**Visual system — "The Dossier."** A warm detective case-file aesthetic. Clay `#C25A38` and clay-deep `#A8472A` as the lead color; warm cream paper card `#FFFDF8`; ink for type; ink-secondary `#6E675B`; hairline `#E6DDCC`; wax-seal red `#B23A2C`; an amber highlighter accent. Display type is **Fraunces** (serif), body is **Inter**, labels/code are **Geist Mono**. Throughout: cinematic, shallow depth of field, soft film grain, calm and confident pacing — never frantic. Motion is restrained and deliberate; the product's whole point is honesty, so the film should feel grounded, not hyped.

**Recurring motifs** to thread across shots so the cut feels coherent: the tilting 3D dossier folder with a folder tab and a wax seal; receipt/stamp "Exhibit A–F" chips; an amber highlighter swipe landing on corroborated claims; redaction bars over the "Couldn't verify" block; a word-by-word reveal as each claim streams in; a live WEB_SEARCH / FETCH activity log; a SEARCHING → FETCHING → SYNTHESIZING phase indicator with animated connector lines.

**Tone rule for every line:** sharp, specific, grounded. No "revolutionary," no "game-changing," no fabricated numbers. Market figures (Crayon / Klue / Kompyte ~$15k–$30k/yr) are illustrative only and should not be stated as fact on screen.

### SHOT 1 — The Pain (0:00–0:16, 16s)

- **On-screen action:** A dim desk at dusk. A laptop shows a generic "AI competitive analysis" tool: confident bullet points filling the screen, no links, no dates. As the camera pushes in, the bullets quietly glitch — one number changes itself, a "fact" rewrites — revealing them as confabulation. A coffee goes cold beside it.
- **Camera move:** Slow dolly-in on the screen, locked and patient.
- **Lighting/mood:** Low warm key, deep shadow, blue screen-glow vs. warm room. Uneasy, quiet.
- **On-screen text (kinetic, Fraunces):** "Confident. Uncited. *Wrong.*"
- **Voiceover:** "AI gives you a competitive teardown in seconds. Confident bullet points. No sources. No dates. No way to tell what's real — so product managers stopped trusting it, and went back to reading every page themselves."

- **Camera move:** Gentle craning rise as the folder settles flat; a slight parallax on the clay glow behind it.
- **Lighting/mood:** Bright, warm, optimistic flip — the dossier is the answer to the dark room before it.
- **On-screen text (Fraunces, large):** "Receipts" / sub: "Competitive teardowns that show their sources."

### SHOT 4 — Watch It Read Real Pages (0:46–1:06, 20s) — *the hero shot*

- **On-screen action:** The cinematic "CaseFileGathering" loading state. A pulsing clay dot. A live activity log slides in row by row in Geist Mono: `SEARCH: "Linear pricing"` → `FETCH: linear.app/pricing` → `FETCH: linear.app/changelog` → `SEARCH: "Linear limitations complaints"`. A phase indicator advances along animated connector lines: **SEARCHING → FETCHING → SYNTHESIZING**. Two model chips flick on: `llama-3.3-70b` (gathering), `gpt-oss-120b` (writing).
- **Camera move:** Slow vertical tracking down the log as new rows arrive, like reading a teletype.
- **Lighting/mood:** Focused, "the machine is working" — but honest work, not theatrics. The log is the proof.
- **On-screen text (overlaid, Fraunces):** "It's reading the real pages. Right now."
- **Voiceover:** "Watch it work. One agent reads the real homepage, the real pricing page, the changelog — and one third-party review, where the honest weaknesses live. Two free models on Groq, two tools. Live, not from memory."

### SHOT 5 — The Cited Teardown (1:06–1:24, 18s)

- **On-screen action:** The dossier fills with six "Exhibits." Claims reveal word-by-word. Each claim carries a receipt-stamp "Exhibit A–F" chip and a source chip linking the exact page. An amber highlighter swipes across two claims marked **high confidence** — corroborated by 2+ sources. We glide past Exhibit A (Positioning) → B (ICP) → C (Pricing) → D (Recent moves) → E (Strengths) → F (Weaknesses).
- **Camera move:** Smooth lateral pan across the exhibit cards, settling on a single claim and its source chip.
- **Lighting/mood:** Satisfying, legible, earned. Paper, ink, stamps — evidence you can click.
- **On-screen text (Geist Mono chip):** "EXHIBIT C · SOURCE: linear.app/pricing"
- **Voiceover:** "Six facets — positioning, who it's for, pricing, recent moves, strengths, weaknesses. Every claim links to the exact page it came from. The amber means two sources agree."

### SHOT 6 — The "Couldn't Verify" Honesty Moment (1:24–1:40, 16s) — *the wedge*

- **On-screen action:** The view scrolls to a distinct block titled **"Couldn't verify."** Three redaction bars sit over short notes — what the agent looked for but could not back with a fetched source. Nothing is invented here; the redaction bars hold, openly. A small line of Geist Mono: "no source ⇒ not asserted."
- **Camera move:** A slow push toward the redaction bars, then a beat of stillness — let the honesty land.
- **Lighting/mood:** Quiet, serious, trustworthy. This is the moment that separates Receipts from every confabulating tool.
- **On-screen text (Fraunces):** "What it *can't* prove, it won't claim."
- **Voiceover:** "And here's the part no other tool shows you. What it couldn't back with a source goes here — visible, never fabricated. The agent defers to you instead of bluffing."

## 4. FALLBACK: SCREEN-RECORDING SHOT LIST

A real product demo is most credible as an actual screen recording. This plan captures the same 2-minute story live in the deployed app.

### Capture settings

- **Resolution / frame rate:** 1080p (1920×1080), 30fps. Record at 60fps if exporting slow pans, deliver 30fps.
- **Cursor:** Enable cursor highlight + click-ripple (e.g. a soft clay-tinted ring). Slow the cursor; move deliberately.
- **Browser:** Clean window, no extensions/bookmarks bar, 100% zoom, light background. Hide notifications and the clock.
- **Audio:** Record VO separately (Section 3) and lay it over the silent capture; do not capture system audio.
- **Subject:** Use a real, well-structured target with a public pricing page and changelog (e.g. Linear) so the activity log and Exhibits populate cleanly. Do a warm-up run first so any cold-start latency is gone.

### Step-by-step

| # | Action | What to highlight | Target |
|---|--------|-------------------|--------|
| 1 | Open the deployed Receipts URL fresh; let the hero (dossier card, wax seal, "No source, no claim.") settle. | Tagline + the one input field. Hover the seal once to show its cursor reaction. | 0:00–0:12 |
| 2 | Click the input. Type the target name + URL slowly. | The single field; keep scope visible — no settings, no login. | 0:12–0:24 |

### Editing notes

- Trim dead air between clicks; keep the live tool-call log full-length — it's the proof and shouldn't be sped up.
- If any single FETCH stalls on the day, it's fine: the "Couldn't verify" block turns that into an on-message moment rather than a failure.
- Cross-fade VO over the held beats at steps 4 and 6. Total runtime: target 1:58–2:00, comfortably under the sub-3-minute requirement.
- Lower-third captions optional; if used, set them in Geist Mono, ink on cream, bottom-left, never covering the activity log or source chips.
